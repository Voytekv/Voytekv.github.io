(Russian: закрытые административно-территориальные образования [ЗАТО]

Definition: A ZATO city (short for "Закрытое административно-территориальное образование" or Closed Administrative-Territorial Formation [ЗАТО]) is a closed town or city in Russia that has restricted access due to its military, scientific, or strategic significance.

What's the difference?

Key features:

1. Access is controlled: Entry is typically restricted to residents, employees, or people with special permits.
2. Often associated with military or nuclear activities: Many ZATO cities have military bases, nuclear facilities, weapons production, or research institutes.
3. Not always marked on maps: Especially during the Soviet era, many of these cities were kept secret and were not labeled on public maps. (However those still exist today.)
4. Code names: Instead of using their real names, they were often referred to using a postal code and a nearby city, like “Tomsk-7” or “Arzamas-16.”
