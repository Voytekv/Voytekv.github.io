GHOST TOWNS

Definition: A ghost town is a town or settlement that has been abandoned or is nearly deserted, often due to economic decline, natural or human-made disasters, or the depletion of resources that once supported the population.

But why loose an antire town?

1. Mining booms and busts: Towns built around gold, silver, or coal mines often thrived during the boom, then emptied when the resources ran out. (Ugolny ruchei, Sadon, kadykcahn...)
2. Changes in transportation routes: When highways or railroads were rerouted, some towns lost vital traffic and business. (Lultin, Charonda...)
3. Natural disasters: Earthquakes, floods, or droughts have caused entire communities to leave. (Kursha-2, Neftegorsk...)
4. Economic collapse or war: Factories closing or conflicts can push residents to relocate. (Tiksi-2, Alykel...)
5. Industrial disasters and incident leading to the non viability of the town (Kyshtym, Verkhnyaya, Namyetkin, Pechenga)

One of the most famous exemple is in Ukraine, Prypiat, who was within the exclusion zone.
